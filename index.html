Purpose: Exploring Machine Learning Techniques for Classification
Author: R.P.M. Kras | Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam

This thesis evaluates the performance of three popular machine learning algorithms—Random Forest, Support Vector Machine (SVM), and Neural Network—across diverse classification tasks.

Key insights include:

  • Random Forest: Exhibited strong stability and robust performance with categorical and numerical data but struggled with imbalanced datasets.
  • SVM: Delivered the best overall accuracy and reliability for smaller datasets, particularly in multi-class classification.
  • Neural Network: Demonstrated potential in predictive tasks but faced challenges due to dataset biases, underscoring its need for specialized datasets or alternative techniques.
This research emphasizes the importance of dataset selection and model optimization, recommending future exploration of unbiased, larger datasets and tailored techniques for more reliable results.

Keywords: Machine Learning, Classification, Random Forest, SVM, Neural Network
